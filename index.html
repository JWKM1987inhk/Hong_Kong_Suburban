Discovering Hong Kong
One MTR station at a time


Tseung Kwan O Line

Surrounded by malls and wide roads, Po Lam is a modern neighborhood. Its numerous parks and access to nature might even be enough to convince you to move.

East Rail Line

The long, tree lined paths leading to an impeccably maintained city park will show you a side of Sheung Shui not commonly seen in this border town.

Island Line

Located on the east side of Hong Kong Island, Sai Wan Ho has all the feeling of the traditional Kowloon side, conveniently located on the island.

Island Line

Shau Kei Wan not only serves as a transport hub to the sunny, southern side of the island, it stands on its own as a shoppers mecca with a plethora of things to see, do, and eat.

East Rail Line

Tai Po is the closest thing that can be considered a "suburb" in Hong Kong. While there is a market that can be explored, come for the things that can not be found in the city like river side bike paths, a railway museum, and a lower key vibe.

Tsuen Wan Line

Sham Shui Po is the handsome, more successful cousin of Mong Kok. Compared to the famous markets in and around Mong Kok, Sham Shui Po's markets have a wider selection of goods, a relatively less crowded atmosphere, and wider alleys.

Kwun Tong Line

Choi Hung is often overlooked as a quick stop before heading to Sai Kung. With an open market and an abundance of cheap places to get a hair cut, the area is certainly worth exploring before catching the minibus to Sai Kung.
